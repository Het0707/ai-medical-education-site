Current Use of AI in Canadian Hospitals

Canadian hospitals are actively integrating Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies into daily clinical workflows. These applications are aimed at improving diagnostic accuracy, optimizing workflow efficiency, and supporting decision-making.

1. Radiology and Imaging

AI-powered tools like computer vision are now used to analyze X-rays, CT scans, and MRIs to detect anomalies such as tumors, fractures, and infections. For instance, hospitals in Ontario have piloted AI systems that assist radiologists in detecting early-stage lung cancer.

2. Predictive Analytics for Diagnostics

Machine learning models are used to predict patient outcomes based on historical data, lab results, and patient vitals. These tools are being used in emergency departments to triage patients and identify those at high risk of deterioration or readmission.

3. Virtual Assistants & Chatbots

Some hospitals have introduced AI chatbots to assist in pre-diagnosis and appointment booking. These tools can collect preliminary data and route patients to the appropriate departments.
